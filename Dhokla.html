Ingredients

For Dhokla Batter:
1 cup gram flour (besan)
1/4 cup semolina (sooji/rava)
1 cup plain yogurt
1/2 teaspoon ginger paste
1/2 teaspoon green chili paste
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon salt (or to taste)
1 teaspoon fruit salt (eno or baking soda)
1 tablespoon oil
For Tempering (Tadka):
2 tablespoons oil
1 teaspoon mustard seeds
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 green chili, chopped
A few curry leaves
A pinch of asafoetida (hing)
For Garnish:
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
Grated coconut (optional)

Instructions

For Dhokla Batter:
Prepare the Batter:
In a mixing bowl, combine gram flour, semolina, yogurt, ginger paste, green chili paste, turmeric powder, salt, and oil. Mix well to form a smooth batter.
Ferment the Batter:
Allow the batter to ferment for about 4-6 hours or overnight. The batter should rise and become slightly fluffy.
Add Fruit Salt:
Before steaming, add fruit salt (eno or baking soda) to the batter and mix gently. The batter will become frothy.
Steam the Dhokla:
Grease a steaming dish or plate. Pour the batter into the dish and steam it for about 15-20 minutes until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean.
For Tempering (Tadka):
Heat Oil:
In a small pan, heat oil.
Add Mustard and Cumin Seeds:
Add mustard seeds and cumin seeds. Allow them to splutter.
Add Aromatics:
Add chopped green chili, curry leaves, and a pinch of asafoetida. Sauté for a minute.
Temper the Dhokla:
Pour this tempering mixture evenly over the steamed dhokla.
Serving:
Cut into Pieces: Allow the tempered dhokla to cool for a few minutes. Cut it into pieces or squares.
Garnish:
Garnish with chopped coriander leaves and grated coconut if desired.
Serve:
Serve dhokla with mint chutney or tamarind chutney.

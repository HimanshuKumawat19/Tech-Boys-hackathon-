Ingredients

1 cup whole black lentils (urad dal)
1 large onion, finely chopped
2 large tomatoes, pureed
1/4 cup butter
1/4 cup cream
1 tablespoon ginger-garlic paste
1-2 green chilies, chopped (adjust to taste)
1 teaspoon cumin seeds
1 teaspoon garam masala
1/2 teaspoon turmeric powder
1 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
Salt to taste
Fresh coriander leaves for garnish
1/4 cup kidney beans (rajma)

Instructions

Soak Lentils and Beans:
Wash urad dal and rajma and soak them together in water overnight or for at least 8 hours.
Cook Lentils and Beans:
In a pressure cooker, cook the soaked urad dal and rajma with enough water and a pinch of salt until they are soft and fully cooked. This may take around 20-25 minutes.
Prepare Tomato Puree:
While the lentils and beans are cooking, puree the tomatoes in a blender.
Cooking the Dal:
In a large pan, heat butter. Add cumin seeds and let them splutter. Add chopped onions and sauté until they become golden brown.
Add Ginger-Garlic Paste and Spices:
Add ginger-garlic paste and chopped green chilies. Sauté for a minute until the raw aroma disappears. Add turmeric powder, red chili powder, and garam masala. Mix well.
Add Tomato Puree:
Add the tomato puree and cook until the oil starts separating from the masala.
Add Cooked Lentils and Beans:
Add the cooked urad dal and rajma to the tomato gravy. Mix well.
Simmer:
Let the dal simmer on low heat for at least 30-40 minutes. You can mash some of the lentils with the back of the spoon to thicken the dal.
Add Cream:
Add cream to the dal and mix well. Adjust salt and spices according to your taste.
Garnish and Serve:
Garnish the dal makhani with fresh coriander leaves. Serve hot with naan, roti, or rice.

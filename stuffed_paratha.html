Ingredients

For the Paratha Dough:
Water (as needed for kneading)
Salt to taste
For the Potato Filling:
3 medium-sized potatoes, boiled and mashed
1 small onion, finely chopped
1 green chili, finely chopped
1/2 teaspoon grated ginger
1/2 teaspoon cumin seeds
1/2 teaspoon garam masala
1/2 teaspoon red chili powder (adjust to taste)
Fresh coriander leaves, chopped
Salt to taste
For Cooking:
Ghee or oil for cooking
2 cups whole wheat flour (atta)

Instructions

For the Paratha Dough:
In a large mixing bowl, combine whole wheat flour and a pinch of salt. Gradually add water and knead the flour to form a smooth, soft dough. Cover the dough and let it rest for at least 15-20 minutes.
For the Potato Filling:
In a mixing bowl, combine boiled and mashed potatoes with chopped onions, green chili, grated ginger, cumin seeds, garam masala, red chili powder, chopped coriander leaves, and salt. Mix the filling thoroughly to ensure even distribution of spices.
For Assembling and Cooking:
Divide the dough into equal-sized balls. Take one dough ball, roll it into a small disc, and place a portion of the potato filling in the center. Seal the edges of the dough, enclosing the filling to form a stuffed ball. Flatten the stuffed ball with your hands and roll it gently into a circular paratha, using some dry flour to prevent sticking. Heat a tawa or griddle over medium heat. Place the rolled paratha on the hot tawa and cook until small bubbles appear on the surface. Flip the paratha, apply ghee or oil on the cooked side, and cook the other side until golden brown. Repeat the process for the remaining dough balls.
Serving:
Serve the hot Aloo Stuffed Parathas with yogurt, pickles, or any chutney of your choice.
